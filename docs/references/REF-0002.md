The RRF paper is three pages long and carries the entire fusion story of our
hybrid search: when you have two rankings of the same corpus (BM25 and vector
similarity, for us), summing 1/(k + rank) per document is a remarkably strong,
calibration-free way to combine them. The paper showed it beating Condorcet
fusion and learned rank combinations on TREC runs.

## Where it lives here

- Hybrid fusion inside a single home (BM25 list + vector list).
- Cross-home fusion when global and project scopes both answer
  (ADR 0112.1; contract test `cross-home-rrf.test.ts`).

The constant k=60 is the paper default. Decay and usage ranking are applied
after fusion — RRF orders relevance; authority signals reorder it.
